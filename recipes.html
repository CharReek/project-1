Peanut Butter & Bananna Birthday Cake

We may all not have the skills of Merry berry and Paul Hollywood but by following this easy recipe you can become a star baker! This recipe is easy to follow using ingredients that can be found in your home, no need for fancy ingredient that cost a fortune. The great thing about this recipe is you can substitute out some of the ingredients so you can tailor the cake to you dogs liking. This cake is the perfect way to spoil your dog and show off you skills at a doggy birthday party! You can get creative and decorate the cake using all your pups favourite treats, Sams personal favourites are the Lilly's kitchen chomp-away chicken bites. Humans can also eat this cake, so if you are feeling brave give it a try!

Prep Time Cook Time Serving Size
30 Minutes 30 minutes 8 Servings

Ingredients

Cake

  • 136g flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp vegtable oil
  • 70g peanut Butter
  • 1 egg
  • 4 Bananans

Frosting

  • 145g plain greek yogurt
  • 70g peanut butter

Instructions

Cake

  1. Pre heat the oven to 180°
  2. Lightly grease a 8” round pan with butter
  3. Combine flour and baking soda in a large bowl.
  4. In another bowl combine vegetable oil, peanut butter and bananas.
  5. Once this is combined, stir in the egg and continue to mix until fully combined.
  6. Mix together the wet and dry ingredients and stir until blended into a mixture.
  7. Pour mixture into the pan you prepared earlier.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Remove the cake from the pan and place on a wire rack until cooled.
  10. One cooled add frosting and decorate as desired.

Frosting

  1. Combine greek yogurt and peanut butter.
  2. Mix until well combined.
  3. Spread over the cake, using as much as desired.

Optional

  1. Decorate with your dogs favourite treats.

Notes

To make a 2 layer cake like the one shown above you will need to double the mixture and use 2 8" pans.

This recipe can be adjusted so it suits your dogs personal tastes. You can subsitute the bananas for carrots, you can even add a drop of honey to increase the sweetness of the cake.

Alternative frostings can be made using 600g of mashed potato and 245g of plain Greek yogurt
